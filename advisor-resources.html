Step-by-step: from inquiry to filing

1. Intake the objective. Separate travel convenience, tax residence change, education access, and contingency “Plan B” goals. Each points to different program types and holding periods.

2. Screen eligibility. Use nationality, source-of-funds profile, and criminal/civil disclosure history to shortlist jurisdictions before discussing fees or timelines.

3. Compare program mechanics. Map minimum investment, refundable vs. non-refundable contributions, real-estate lockups, and dependent rules side by side—our comparison matrices are built for advisor notes, not consumer marketing.

4. Align the professional stack. Confirm which pieces sit with you (balance sheet, trust structure), tax counsel (reporting, exit tax), and immigration counsel or broker (applications, government relations).

5. Document the recommendation. Retain rationale, alternatives considered, and risk factors in the same format you use for securities recommendations.

6. Introduce execution partners. Request a vetted broker introduction through Plan B Residency when you are ready; we do not compete for asset management mandates.

Tax implications

RBI and CBI do not automatically change U.S. tax status, but they can trigger reporting, PFIC questions, and exit-planning conversations. Our briefs summarize common fact patterns and point to where tax counsel must sign off—without offering tax advice.

Client-ready memos

Downloadable outlines help you explain program tradeoffs in the same tone as an IPS appendix: risks, costs, illiquidity, and non-guarantee of approval. Customize with your firm’s branding and supervisory workflow.

Broker introductions

When diligence is complete, we match you to brokers who have executed similar profiles (e.g., entrepreneur vs. salaried executive, trust-funded vs. personal assets). You retain the client; the broker manages government correspondence and filing packages.
