The future of European Golden Visas

Residency-by-investment in the EU is under sustained political and regulatory scrutiny. This note is for advisors who need a sober committee narrative—not a sales deck from a program promoter.

By [AUTHOR NAME], [CREDENTIALS] | Last Updated: March 15, 2026 | Reviewed by Advisory Board

Why “Golden Visa” is a moving target

Several member states still operate residence routes tied to capital transfer, real assets, or fund subscriptions, but the EU institutions have repeatedly pushed for stricter alignment with security, anti–money laundering, and tax-transparency norms. For your clients, that means published rules, processing times, and minimum-stay tests can change between the date of a pitch deck and the date of filing. Any internal memo should state explicitly that approval is not guaranteed and that statute beats marketing collateral.

Physical presence and substance

Even where programs advertise low stay requirements, tax residence and social-insurance nexus in the host country are separate questions. Committees should expect more emphasis on genuine ties—leases, economic activity, and time on the ground—as political pressure continues. Pair this article with tax counsel whenever a client conflates “having a card” with “being taxed only there.”

Implications for diligence and disclosures

When investment migration sits alongside securities advice, document why a given program was presented, what alternatives were considered, and how promoter economics were disclosed. That posture is consistent with Reg BI–style care obligations when referrals are in scope. See our memo on Reg BI documentation for investment migration referrals.

U.S. clients: keep tax in a separate lane

European residence does not by itself resolve U.S. tax status for citizens or for those who meet U.S. substantial presence. Use How a Golden Visa affects U.S. tax residency as the handoff scaffold for CPAs and tax attorneys.
